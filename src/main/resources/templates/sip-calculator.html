What is a SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount in a mutual fund at regular intervals — typically monthly. Instead of timing the market with a lump sum, SIPs spread your investment over time, averaging out the purchase cost and reducing risk.

How is SIP Maturity Value Calculated?

The future value of a SIP is calculated using the compound interest formula for an annuity:

FV = P × ((1 + r)⊃n⊃ − 1) / r × (1 + r)

Where P is the monthly investment, r is the monthly rate (annual return ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the total number of months. All calculations run in your browser — nothing is sent or stored.

The Power of Long-Term SIP

  • Rupee cost averaging: Buying more units when prices are low and fewer when high, reducing average cost over time.
  • Compounding: Returns earned each period are reinvested, so your corpus grows exponentially over long durations.
  • Discipline: Automating a fixed monthly investment removes the temptation to time the market.
  • Flexibility: You can start with as little as ₹500/month and increase the amount as your income grows.

Frequently Asked Questions

What return rate should I use?

Common assumptions: large-cap equity funds at 10–12%, mid/small-cap at 12–15%, debt funds at 6–8%. Use conservative estimates for long-term planning — actual returns will vary year to year.

Does this account for step-up SIPs?

This calculator assumes a fixed monthly amount throughout the tenure. For step-up SIPs (where you increase the amount annually), use the SIP entry in the Net Worth Calculator which supports annual step-up rates.

Does duration matter more than the amount?

Both matter, but time has a disproportionate effect because of compounding. Investing ₹5,000/month for 30 years typically generates far more wealth than ₹20,000/month for 10 years at the same return rate.

Is this applicable to mutual funds only?

The formula works for any investment with regular periodic contributions and a compounded return — mutual fund SIPs, recurring deposits, PPF top-ups, and so on.

Is my data saved anywhere?

No. All calculations happen in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server or stored.
