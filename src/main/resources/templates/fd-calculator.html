What is a Fixed Deposit?

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a financial instrument offered by banks and NBFCs where you deposit a lump sum for a fixed tenure at a predetermined interest rate. Unlike savings accounts, the rate is locked in for the entire tenure, giving you predictable, guaranteed returns.

How is FD Maturity Value Calculated?

FDs use the compound interest formula:

A = P × (1 + r/n)⊃n×t⊃

Where P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate (as a decimal), n is the number of times interest is compounded per year, and t is the tenure in years. More frequent compounding yields a slightly higher maturity value for the same nominal rate.

Compounding Frequency Explained

  • Monthly (12×/yr): Interest is calculated and added to principal every month — highest effective yield.
  • Quarterly (4×/yr): The most common frequency for bank FDs in India.
  • Half-yearly (2×/yr): Interest compounded every six months.
  • Yearly (1×/yr): Interest added once a year — lowest effective yield for the same nominal rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What interest rate should I use?

Check your bank's current FD rate card for the tenure you plan to invest. Rates in India typically range from 5.5% to 9% depending on the bank, tenure, and whether you are a senior citizen (who often get an additional 0.25–0.5%).

Is FD interest taxable?

Yes. FD interest is added to your income and taxed at your applicable slab rate. Banks deduct TDS at 10% if total interest in a financial year exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens). This calculator shows pre-tax maturity values.

What is the difference between cumulative and non-cumulative FDs?

In a cumulative FD, interest is compounded and paid at maturity (what this calculator shows). In a non-cumulative FD, interest is paid out periodically — monthly, quarterly, or annually — making it suitable for regular income needs.

Can I break an FD before maturity?

Most banks allow premature withdrawal with a small penalty (typically 0.5–1% reduction in the applicable rate). The maturity value will be lower than projected.
