What is an EMI?

An Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is the fixed amount you pay your lender every month until the loan is fully repaid. Each payment covers a portion of the principal and the interest accrued during that month.

How is EMI Calculated?

The standard EMI formula is:

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)⊃n⊃ / ((1 + r)⊃n⊃ − 1)

Where P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the number of monthly instalments. All calculations run entirely in your browser — nothing is sent or stored.

How to Lower Your EMI?

  • Increase the tenure: Spreading repayment over more months reduces each instalment, though total interest paid goes up.
  • Make a larger down payment: A smaller principal directly reduces the EMI.
  • Negotiate a lower rate: Even a 0.5% reduction can meaningfully cut both monthly payments and total interest.
  • Make prepayments: Lump-sum payments reduce outstanding principal and can shorten the loan term.

Frequently Asked Questions

What factors affect my EMI?

Three things determine your EMI: the loan amount (higher = higher EMI), the interest rate (higher = higher EMI), and the tenure (longer = lower EMI but higher total interest paid over the life of the loan).

Does a longer tenure always mean a lower EMI?

Yes, but at a cost. While a longer tenure reduces your monthly payment, you end up paying significantly more total interest. Use the calculator to compare the trade-off between EMI and total interest for different tenures.

Can I reduce my EMI after taking a loan?

Lump-sum prepayments reduce the outstanding principal, which decreases future interest. You can either let the EMI stay the same (shortening the tenure) or request your lender to recalculate a lower EMI.

Does this work for all loan types?

Yes — the formula applies to home loans, car loans, personal loans, and any fixed-rate instalment loan. Floating-rate loans will see their EMI change whenever the base interest rate is revised.
